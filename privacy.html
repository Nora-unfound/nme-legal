Privacy Policy

N=Me App · Last updated: May 31, 2026

N=Me ("we", "our", "the app") is a personal health tracking app. This Privacy Policy explains what data the app collects, how it is used, and your rights.

1. Data We Collect

N=Me collects only the data you actively provide or authorize:

  • Profile name — An optional name you enter in the app. It is stored locally on your device.
  • Health data — Steps, heart rate, sleep, HRV, and other HealthKit metrics you authorize. This data is read from the Apple Health app on your device.
  • Location — Your current coordinates, used while the app is open to request local weather and air quality. N=Me stores environmental values, not location history.
  • Habit and lifestyle records — Data you manually enter into the app (e.g., mood, energy, custom items you create).
  • Lab investigations — Experiments and reports you design in the Lab feature (e.g., conditions, observations, outcomes). Stored locally and optionally synced to iCloud.

2. How We Use Your Data

  • To power the core features of the app: trend analysis, correlation discovery, and personal experiments.
  • To export or merge-import your own backup files when you choose to do so.
  • We do not use your data for advertising, marketing, or analytics.
  • We do not sell your data to any third party.

3. Data Storage

Your data is stored as follows:

  • Locally on your device — in a private SQLite database.
  • Health data — imported from Apple Health and kept on this device. It is not uploaded by N=Me.
  • iCloud (optional) — If you enable iCloud sync in Settings, your custom items, manual records, lab investigations, and environment records are synced to your private iCloud database, managed by Apple. Health data is never uploaded by N=Me. You can disable sync or delete iCloud data at any time from Settings → Data Management.
  • Backup files — if you export data, the backup file is saved or shared only to the destination you choose through the iOS share sheet.

N=Me does not operate its own servers. Your data stays on your device unless you enable iCloud sync.

4. Data Sharing

We do not sell your personal data or use it for advertising. To import environment data, N=Me sends your current coordinates to weather and air quality providers such as Open-Meteo. N=Me stores the returned environmental values, not your coordinate history.

5. Third-Party Services

  • Apple HealthKit — used to read health metrics. Governed by Apple's Privacy Policy.
  • Apple iCloud (CloudKit) — used for optional data sync when you enable it in Settings. Governed by Apple's Privacy Policy.
  • Apple App Store — used to process in-app subscription purchases. Governed by Apple's Privacy Policy.
  • Open-Meteo — used to fetch weather and air quality values for your current location.

6. Your Rights

  • Access your data — All your data is visible within the app.
  • Export your data — Use Settings → Data Management → Export Data to download a full copy.
  • Delete local data — Use Profile → Delete All Local Data to erase data from this device.
  • Revoke Health access — Go to iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Health → N=Me to remove access at any time.
  • Revoke Location access — Go to iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Location Services → N=Me.
  • Delete iCloud data — Use Settings → Data Management → Delete iCloud Data to remove synced data from Apple's servers while keeping local data intact.

7. Children's Privacy

N=Me is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect data from children.

8. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The "Last updated" date at the top of this page reflects the most recent revision. Continued use of the app after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
